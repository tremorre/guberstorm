Guberstorm

This website is meant to store my thoughts on the Revolutions Custom Standard deck named Guberstorm, originally developed by Lih but developed into a faster though somewhat less resilient deck by me, despite one major relevant nerf of a card and an additional ban of a key sideboard card.

How The Deck Wins

The deck is built on and named after the card often abbreviated as Guber.

Most of the card is self-explanatory, but for those who aren't coming here from Revolutions Custom Standard, the make a pledge mechanic effectively gets you an effect each time except the first. You have a choice of which effect you want, but in this deck, we always want Vishanki

If one is able to have 3 copies of this effect on the battlefield at once, then all 3-mana spells can be cast mana-neutral, because each will generate 3 treasures which can be spent on the next cast. The original way to take advantage of this was to play many card draw spells, some of which are strongly mana-positive, and end up with a finite but very large number of treasures. I spotted the card Seer of the Aspirants which is able, with three Gubers in play, to cast itself from play repeatedly, and each time getting a surveil. We'll get into the details later, but suffice it to say that this is sufficient to win the game in almost every circumstance. In particular, if we can get a 4th Guber in play, we can generate infinite treasures by casting Seer repeatedly, and with infinite surveils, it is generally easy to get to this point.

Making the Combo

What makes this deck a 3-card combo, which is feasible, rather than a 4-card combo, which is generally not, is the card Reflected Echo. If you start your turn with a Guber in play, you can cast Reflected Echo on it, and make 2 treasures, and then spend them and 2 more mana to flashback the card, you end up with 3 Gubers in play and 3 treasures which you can use to start the unlimited chain of 3-mana spells.
